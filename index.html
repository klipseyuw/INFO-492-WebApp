Agentic AI Systems in Logistics Cybersecurity

Autonomous Threat Detection & Mitigation for Global Supply Chain Resilience

Group 3: Kenta Lipsey, Hanrui Tang, Brian Yuan

Course: INFO 492 B | Date: September 27, 2025

Read Thesis Explore Ecosystem

Thesis Statement

The logistics industry is uniquely susceptible to cyber-physical attacks, ransomware, and supply chain vulnerabilities from third-party vendors. Agentic AI systems are essential for guaranteeing the operational resilience of the global industry by autonomously detecting and mitigating threats in near real-time.

However, this transformative potential is critically threatened by the inherent risk amplification within agentic systems, demanding the urgent adoption of robust governance and layered security frameworks to prevent a compromised AI agent from spreading sophisticated malicious commands and causing widespread, cascading disruption across the entire supply chain network.

Critical Vulnerabilities

Cyber-physical attacks, ransomware, and third-party vendor risks create unprecedented attack surfaces

AI-Driven Defense

Agentic AI systems provide autonomous, real-time threat detection and mitigation capabilities

Governance Challenge

Risk amplification in AI systems requires robust frameworks to prevent cascading failures

Industry Context: The Logistics Ecosystem

Logistics functions as a multi-party network where physical goods and digital information flow in parallel
